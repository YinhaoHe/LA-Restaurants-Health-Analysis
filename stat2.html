How many restaurants in LA? What are their types?

Data Explanation

  • March 10,2019

Numbers of facilities per facility type

The chart categorizes the LA restaurants in different categories.Permits in the restaurants are now based on risk levels, which are determined by type of food and preparation steps. In general, the more complex the menu/preparation of foods are, the higher the risk level.

    Some Explainations:
  • Restaurant Seats --> The size of the restaurants is defined by how many seats are available in the restaurant, the data in this graph does not categorize the sizes of the restaurants.
  • Low Risk --> Restaurants are permitted to sell limited preparation steps of foods, such as hot dogs, heating of individually pre-packaged ready-to-eat foods without opening of the package.These types of operations are inspected once a year.
  • Moderate Risk --> Restaurants are permitted to receive, store, prepare, cold holding, and serve foods, such as baking bread, frying donuts. These operations receive 1 routine inspection and 1 educational visit each year with the exception of schools which receive 2 routine inspections.
  • High Risk --> Restaurants are permitted to do operations with complex food preparation steps, such as cooking, cutting, hot holding. These operations receive 2 routine inspections and 1 educational visit each year.

Suggestions are stated below:

  • For government use, it helps to realize the scope of restaurants in each category and to provide suggestions for centralized management on specific type of restaurant.
  • For commercial use, it helps to estimate the scope of current competitive market and to suggest the customers' current tastes in LA.

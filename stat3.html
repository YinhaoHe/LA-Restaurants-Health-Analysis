What is the average health score based on restaurant types?

Data Explanation

  • March 10,2019

Average score per facility type

The chart shows numbers of average health scores in different facility types. Permits in the restaurants are now based on risk levels, which are determined by type of food and preparation steps. In general, the more complex the menu/preparation of foods are, the higher the risk level.

    Some Explainations:
  • (minimum number-maximum number) --> It indicates the sizes of the restaurant. For example, "RESTAURANT(61-150)SEATS HIGH RISK", it refers to the restaurants which have 61-150 seats in their store areas, and serving high risk foods.
  • Low Risk --> Restaurants are permitted to sell limited preparation steps of foods, such as hot dogs, heating of individually pre-packaged ready-to-eat foods without opening of the package.These types of operations are inspected once a year.
  • Moderate Risk --> Restaurants are permitted to receive, store, prepare, cold holding, and serve foods, such as baking bread, frying donuts. These operations receive 1 routine inspection and 1 educational visit each year with the exception of schools which receive 2 routine inspections.
  • High Risk --> Restaurants are permitted to do operations with complex food preparation steps, such as cooking, cutting, hot holding. These operations receive 2 routine inspections and 1 educational visit each year.

Suggestions are stated below:

  • For government use, it helps to compare health scores between different facility types and find out which type of restaurants need to be focused and strengthened.
  • For commercial use, it helps to raise people's attention on the restaurants health scores, and to pay more attention on those with low grades, such as private school cafeteria does not have relatively high health score compared to others.
